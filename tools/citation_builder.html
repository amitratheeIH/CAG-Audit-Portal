Citation formats explained

Four styles are generated. Use whichever matches the context — academic papers, legal documents, web references, or court pleadings.

1. Formal / Legal style
Report of the Comptroller and Auditor General of India on Degradation of Kshipra River, Madhya Pradesh, Report No. 6 of 2023, Laid on the Table of Madhya Pradesh Vidhan Sabha on 12 August 2023.
Used in court pleadings, official documents, and legal references. Full formal title. No abbreviation.
2. Academic / APA style
Comptroller and Auditor General of India. (2023). Degradation of Kshipra River, Madhya Pradesh (Report No. 6 of 2023). CAG of India.
Follows APA 7th edition pattern for government reports. Used in academic papers and research.
3. Short / Footnote style
CAG Report No. 6/2023 (Madhya Pradesh) — Kshipra River Degradation, Para 2.3, p.45.
Compact form for footnotes and in-text references. Includes para and page for paragraph-level citations.
4. Hindi / Bilingual style
भारत के नियंत्रक एवं महालेखापरीक्षक (2023)। कुसिपरा नदी के क्षरण पर रिपोर्ट, मध्य प्रदेश। रिपोर्ट संख्या 6, 2023।
Hindi citation for Hindi-language documents and bilingual portals. Uses Devanagari punctuation (। instead of .).
Chapter level — additional elements
CAG of India. (2023). Chapter 2: Stamp Duty Collection. In Report No. 6 of 2023 on Kshipra River Degradation. CAG of India.
"In [Report]" format used for chapter-level citation — same as citing a chapter in an edited book.
Paragraph level — most granular
CAG of India, Report No. 6 of 2023 (Madhya Pradesh), Para 2.3 — Short collection of stamp duty, p.45.
Used for ATN references, PAC documents, court petitions citing specific audit findings.
